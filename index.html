What Did I Learn ?


A pronoun is used in the place of a noun. It substitutes the noun in a paragraph or piece of writing to avoid repetition of the noun.

  1. Personal Pronouns: Personal pronouns refer to a person, animal, place and thing.
    • I will see you tomorrow.
    • Give these books to them.
  2. Possessive Pronouns: Possessive pronouns are used to indicate or show possession of something by someone.
    • Is this your pen? No, it's mine.
    • The book is hers; she lent it to me yesterday.
  3. Demonstrative Pronouns: Demonstrative pronouns point out a thing or a person in a sentence.
    • This is my favourite book.
    • That is my favourite book.
    • These are the keys to the car.
    • Those are the houses we looked at yesterday.
  4. Relative Pronouns: Relative pronouns help us to connect one clause of the sentence to another.
    • She is the student whose artwork won the competition.
    • I know someone who can fix your car.
  5. Reflexive Pronoun: Reflexive pronouns reflect the actions of the subject.
    • They enjoyed themselves at the party.
    • I locked myself inside the house.
  6. Interrogative Pronouns: Interrogative pronouns are used to inquire about something or someone.
    • Who is your best friend?
    • Which is your favourite subject?
